Global Warming

Since the Industrial Revolution, the global annual temperature has increased in total by a little more than 1 degree Celsius. Between 1880 and 1980, it rose on average by 0.07 degrees Celsius every 10 years.

Forced Migration

While worldwide more than 250 million people live outside their home country, it is the estimated 65 million people who have been displaced by violence and conflict.

Extremism

Teenagers can be at greater risk because they are more independent, exploring new things and pushing boundaries as they grow and discover more about their identity, faith and sense of belonging.

Income Inequality

Inequalities of wealth, income, and consumption can harm economic growth in the long run by hindering educational opportunities, human capital formation and intergenerational mobility.
